Projects

Selected engineering and research work across biomedical IoT, robotics, control, and power systems.

  • All
  • Biomedical & Instrumentation
  • Robotics & Control

NeuroGuard

Wearable in-ear EEG seizure-detection device, in development for the Hakeem Academy Annual Competition 2026 (leading a 4-student team). BioAmp EXG Pill + ESP32 acquisition, a 3D-printed PDMS earpiece with Ag/AgCl electrodes, and a Firebase IoT alert pipeline.

Beta-Zero Line-Following Robot

Autonomous line follower on an ESP32 platform (16-element IR array, VNH2SP30 driver). A controller comparison study (PID, LQR, MPC) with full motor system identification in MATLAB/Simulink. Digital Signals course project, with ongoing work toward a paper.

Air Levitation Control System

Nonlinear ping-pong-ball levitation rig: modeled the plant, ran system identification in MATLAB/Simulink, designed a PID controller, and validated stable real-time levitation with an Arduino and IR sensors.

CNC Plotter

A microcontroller-driven CNC plotter that draws shapes, built for the Embedded Systems Laboratory.

Industrial Environment Monitoring IoT

A 5-sensor IoT system (ESP32 + DHT22, BMP280, MPU-6050, MQ-135) tracking temperature, humidity, pressure, vibration, and air quality, with a real-time cloud dashboard over MQTT and RC signal-conditioning circuits. Transducer Technologies course project.

Conveyor Digital Twin

A digital twin and semi-automated priority-fill conveyor control system for Jordania Food Industries, developed after an on-site factory visit (team project).

PV–Battery DC Nanogrid (10 kW)

A 10 kW PV-battery DC nanogrid designed and simulated in MATLAB/Simulink with cascaded PI control (inner current / outer voltage loops). Boost and bidirectional Class-C converters with Bode-based design (60° phase margin). Power Electronics course project.

Human Body Mass Scale Meter

A load-cell precision body scale (0–120 kg, ±0.05 kg) with Wheatstone-bridge signal conditioning and Multisim simulation. Engineering Measurement & Signal Processing course project.
